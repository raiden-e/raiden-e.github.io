## Background

I started as a developer and infrastructure engineer (PowerShell, Python, networking), but my Business Informatics studies — especially modules like Consultative Selling during my semester abroad at Western Carolina University — sparked something bigger. I realized I love the human side: understanding pain points, discussing solutions, and making technology work for people, not just systems.

At SITA's Program Office, I coordinated software rollouts (payment solution migrations across airports in 5+ countries), developed GitHub Copilot integrations, set up internal tools (SharePoint, Kimai), and moderated workshops between business, IT, and data teams. I want to be where solutions meet customers — advising, demoing, and closing deals.

My bachelor's thesis examines AI-powered video generation from PDFs, comparing pipeline avatar systems (HeyGen, Synthesia) against end-to-end diffusion models (Veo 3, Sora 2). It covers the full picture: technical architecture (VAE, GAN, Transformer, Diffusion), business impact (cost efficiency, the shift from "Creator" to "Reviewer"), and the regulatory landscape (EU AI Act, DSGVO, C2PA, Human-in-the-Loop). A concrete look at how AI transforms content creation - and the responsibilities that come with it.
